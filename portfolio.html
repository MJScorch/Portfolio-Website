Project 01 · In Progress

Letterboxd Recommendation Engine

Personal movie recommender trained on Letterboxd export data. Combines NLP text embeddings of film descriptions with SVD over the user–film rating matrix. The goal is recommendations that feel personal, not just popular.

Linear Algebra II covered SVD from first principles. The same decomposition is how collaborative filtering actually works — this project is that theory applied to real data.

Project 03 · Planned

Ontario Fish Identifier

You photograph a fish, the model identifies the species and tells you what to do with it — habitat, Ontario legal size limits, keep or release. Snap a picture on the water and get an answer in seconds.

I fish. That's the whole origin story. No other math student's portfolio has this project, and the interview answer writes itself.

Under the hood

Fine-tuned CNN using transfer learning on a pretrained ResNet. The heavy lifting — low-level feature detection — is already learned. Fine-tuning adapts the final layers to fish species classification specifically, which means reasonable results without an enormous dataset.

§ 04 — Coursework

The math

Optimization
LP, IP, NLP — simplex method, duality theory, convexity. Not just "here's how to run the solver" but the theory behind why the simplex works and what strong duality actually means. min cᵀx s.t. Ax ≤ b, x ≥ 0 Directly applicable to how constrained ML training is formulated.
Linear Algebra I & II
Vector spaces, eigendecomposition, singular value decomposition. Proof-level — not just computation but why A = UΣVᵀ exists and what the geometry means. A = U Σ Vᵀ, Σ = diag(σ₁, …, σₙ) The theoretical basis for PCA, recommendation systems, and low-rank approximation.
Introduction to Proofs
Fermat's little theorem, CRT, RSA public-key crypto, contradiction, truth tables, set theory. The course that changes how you think, not just what you know. aᵖ⁻¹ ≡ 1 (mod p), gcd(a, p) = 1
Calculus I & II
Rigorous single and multivariable calculus. Limits, series convergence, integration. The foundation for understanding loss surfaces, gradient descent, and convergence. ∇L(w) = (1/n) Xᵀ(Xw − y)
